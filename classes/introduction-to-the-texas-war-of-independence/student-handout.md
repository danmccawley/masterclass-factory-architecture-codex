## Learning Objectives
- TLO: Describe the significance of the Texas War of Independence in American history, including its causes, key events, and outcomes.
- ELO: Identify the geographical region of Texas and its neighboring territories in the early 19th century, including demographic and political context.
- ELO: Summarize the initial conditions in Texas under Mexican rule, focusing on political, social, and cultural tensions leading to the revolution.
- ELO: Explain the key causes and motivations behind the Texas War of Independence, highlighting differing perspectives of Anglo settlers and Mexican authorities.
- ELO: Analyze the major battles and leadership during the Texas Revolution, emphasizing the role of Sam Houston and the Battle of San Jacinto.
- ELO: Interpret the Texas Declaration of Independence and its significance as a primary source document.
- ELO: Describe the formation and characteristics of the Republic of Texas, including its constitution and policies on slavery and rights.
- ELO: Evaluate the contested historical interpretations and field disagreements regarding the Texas Revolution, including the role of slavery and political motivations.

## Key Terms
- texas war of independence: A conflict from October 1835 to April 1836 in which Texian settlers fought to gain independence from Mexican rule. Why it matters: It marks the creation of the Republic of Texas and shaped the territorial and political landscape of the United States.
- texian: A term used to describe Anglo-American settlers and residents of Texas during the revolution period. Why it matters: Understanding who the Texians were helps explain the cultural and political tensions leading to the revolution.
- republic of texas: The independent nation established by Texians after winning the war, existing from 1836 to 1845. Why it matters: Its constitution and policies influenced regional politics and the eventual annexation of Texas by the U.S.
- texas declaration of independence: The formal document adopted on March 2, 1836, declaring Texas independent from Mexico. Why it matters: It provides primary evidence of the reasons for revolution and the legal basis for Texas’s independence.
- sam houston: Leader of Texian forces and commander at the decisive Battle of San Jacinto. Why it matters: His leadership was crucial to Texian victory and the establishment of the Republic of Texas.
- battle of san jacinto: The final and decisive battle of the Texas Revolution where Texian forces defeated the Mexican army. Why it matters: It effectively ended the war and secured Texas independence.
- coahuila y tejas: The Mexican state that included Texas during the early 19th century. Why it matters: Its political structure and laws affected settlers and contributed to tensions leading to the revolution.
- anglo-american settlers: Immigrants from the United States who settled in Texas under Mexican rule. Why it matters: Their cultural differences and resistance to Mexican laws were central causes of the conflict.
- mexican centralist policies: Government actions that centralized power in Mexico City, reducing autonomy for states like Texas. Why it matters: These policies angered settlers and fueled demands for independence.
- tejanos: Mexican residents of Texas who often had complex loyalties during the revolution. Why it matters: Their experiences highlight the diverse perspectives and contested narratives of the conflict.
