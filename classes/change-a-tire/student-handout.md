# Student Handout: change a tire

Class tier: Standard class
Bernard support: ask Bernard for clarification, examples, translation, quiz help, or a deeper explanation. Bernard must stay inside the approved source boundary.

## Learning Objectives
- TLO: Safely prepare to change a vehicle tire using the vehicle guidance, the work area, and basic safety checks.
- TLO: Remove a damaged or flat wheel and install the spare or replacement wheel using the correct basic sequence.
- TLO: Tighten wheel fasteners in the correct pattern and complete the immediate post-change safety checks before driving.
- TLO: Decide when a tire change is not appropriate and when roadside help or professional service is the safer choice.
- ELO: Identify common tools and items used in a basic tire change, such as the jack, lug wrench, spare, and warning devices.
- ELO: Locate and follow the vehicle-specific instructions for jack points, spare-tire limits, and wheel-fastener guidance.
- ELO: Set up the scene to reduce risk, including stopping location, visibility, vehicle stability, and occupant safety.
- ELO: Recognize key hazards during a tire change, including traffic, unstable ground, slipping, pinching, and incorrect lifting points.
- ELO: Use simple personal safety measures appropriate to the task and conditions.
- ELO: Perform basic pre-lift checks, such as parking position, brake setting, transmission position, and wheel blocking if applicable.
- ELO: Loosen wheel fasteners at the correct stage of the process without forcing or damaging them.
- ELO: Raise and support the vehicle only as directed for a temporary wheel change.
- ELO: Remove the wheel, handle it safely, and inspect for obvious issues that would stop the task.
- ELO: Mount the spare or replacement wheel and hand-start fasteners to avoid cross-threading.
- ELO: Snug and tighten fasteners in a crisscross or vehicle-directed pattern, then lower the vehicle and complete final tightening as directed.
- ELO: Check simple post-change items, such as spare condition limits, tool stowage, removed wheel storage, and next-step service needs.

## Key Terms
- jack: A mechanical device used to lift a vehicle off the ground to allow tire removal and replacement. Why it matters: Essential for safely raising the vehicle to access the wheel without causing damage or injury.
- lug nuts: Fasteners that secure the wheel to the vehicle's hub. Why it matters: Proper loosening and tightening of lug nuts ensure the wheel is safely attached and prevents accidents.
- spare tire: A replacement tire carried in the vehicle for temporary use when a regular tire is damaged or flat. Why it matters: Allows the driver to continue driving safely until a permanent repair or replacement can be made.
- tread depth: The measurement of the depth of the grooves in a tire's tread pattern. Why it matters: Indicates tire wear; insufficient tread depth reduces traction and increases risk of accidents.
- penny test: A simple method to check tire tread depth by inserting a penny into the tread grooves. Why it matters: Helps determine if a tire needs replacement to maintain safe driving conditions.
- wheel wedges: Blocks placed against a vehicle’s tires to prevent it from rolling while changing a tire. Why it matters: Enhances vehicle stability and safety during the tire change process.
- crisscross pattern: A sequence for tightening lug nuts by alternating across the wheel in a diagonal pattern. Why it matters: Ensures even pressure on the wheel for secure fastening and prevents warping or damage.
- vehicle-specific jack points: Designated locations on a vehicle frame where the jack should be placed for safe lifting. Why it matters: Using correct jack points prevents vehicle damage and reduces risk of jack slipping or collapse.
- wheel fasteners: Components such as lug nuts or bolts that hold the wheel onto the vehicle hub. Why it matters: Proper handling and tightening are critical for wheel security and vehicle safety.
- post-change safety checks: Inspections and actions performed after installing a spare tire, including checking tire condition and securing tools. Why it matters: Ensures the vehicle is safe to drive and that all equipment is properly stored.
- parking brake: A mechanism that locks the vehicle’s wheels to prevent movement when parked. Why it matters: Used during tire changes to stabilize the vehicle and prevent rolling.
- wheel blocking: Placing objects like wedges behind or in front of tires to prevent vehicle movement. Why it matters: Increases safety by stabilizing the vehicle during tire removal and installation.
- cross-threading: Incorrect alignment of lug nuts when screwing them onto wheel studs, causing damage to threads. Why it matters: Avoiding cross-threading prevents damage to fasteners and ensures secure wheel attachment.
- tire load index: A numerical code indicating the maximum load a tire can safely carry. Why it matters: Important for selecting replacement tires that meet vehicle safety and performance requirements.
- speed rating: A letter code that indicates the maximum safe speed a tire can sustain. Why it matters: Ensures replacement tires are suitable for the vehicle’s operating speeds.
- warning devices: Items such as hazard triangles or flares used to alert other drivers during roadside tire changes. Why it matters: Increase visibility and safety by warning approaching traffic of the stopped vehicle.
- terminal learning objective: The main capability learners should demonstrate by the end of the class. Why it matters: It anchors the lesson around outcomes instead of loose topic coverage.
- enabling learning objective: A supporting skill or idea learners need before they can meet the terminal objective. Why it matters: It turns a big outcome into teachable steps.
- source verification: An independent check that claims and citations are supported by the approved corpus. Why it matters: It keeps fabricated or unsupported material out of the class.
