## Research Gaps to Resolve
- Detailed demographic and socioeconomic data on Texican settlers, including diversity within the group and their economic activities, is limited in the current sources.
- Comprehensive accounts of daily life, cultural practices, and social structures of Texicans beyond land settlement and political roles are insufficiently covered.
- In-depth exploration of Texican interactions with various Native American tribes, including alliances, conflicts, and cultural exchange, lacks detailed primary evidence in the provided sources.
- Clarification on the distinctions and overlaps between Texicans, Texians, and other groups during transitional periods is needed to resolve terminological ambiguities.
- More primary source narratives from Texicans themselves, such as letters, diaries, or oral histories, would strengthen the learner's understanding of their perspectives.
