## Research Gaps to Resolve
- Detailed ethnographic and sociocultural descriptions of Texican settlement patterns and daily lifestyle are missing or insufficiently detailed in the current sources.
- There is a lack of comprehensive primary source narratives specifically authored by Texicans themselves, limiting direct insight into their perspectives.
- The contested nature of the term 'Texican' and its evolution over time requires more explicit historical linguistic and cultural analysis.
- Sources with no extractable text (e.g., s6, s8) limit the ability to verify or incorporate their content fully.
- More balanced perspectives on the interactions between Texicans and indigenous peoples, as well as Anglo-American settlers, are needed to avoid oversimplification.
