Class tier: Standard class

## Learning Objectives
- TLO: Identify who the Texicans were and their significance in Texas history
- ELO: Define the term 'Texicans' with historical context and clarify distinctions from 'Texians' and 'Texans'
- ELO: Describe the origins and early settlement patterns of the Texicans in Spanish Texas
- ELO: Explain the socio-political context of the Texicans during the Mexican War of Independence and Texas Revolution, including their interactions with Mexican authorities and Anglo settlers

## Key Terms
- texicans: Early settlers in Spanish Texas with cultural ties to Mexico and Spain, distinct from Anglo-American settlers; involved in political and military events during Mexican Texas and Texas independence. Why it matters: Understanding Texicans clarifies the cultural and political dynamics in early Texas history and their role in shaping the region's identity.
- texians: Anglo-American settlers in Texas during the early 19th century who identified as residents of Texas, especially during the Texas Revolution. Why it matters: Differentiating Texians from Texicans helps explain the socio-political tensions and conflicts in Texas' path to independence.
- texans: Modern term for residents of Texas, encompassing all cultural and ethnic groups within the state. Why it matters: Recognizing the evolution from Texicans and Texians to Texans shows how Texas identity has broadened over time.
- spanish texas: The territory of Texas under Spanish colonial rule before Mexican independence, where early Texican settlers established communities. Why it matters: Spanish Texas provides the historical setting for the origins of the Texicans and their cultural heritage.
- mexican war of independence: The conflict (1810–1821) through which Mexico gained independence from Spain, impacting Texas and its inhabitants including the Texicans. Why it matters: This war shaped the political context in which Texicans lived and influenced their alliances and status.
- texas revolution: The 1835–1836 conflict in which Texas settlers, including Texians and some Texicans, fought for independence from Mexico. Why it matters: Understanding the Texas Revolution highlights the complex loyalties and tensions involving Texicans during this pivotal period.
- anglo settlers: English-speaking settlers from the United States who migrated to Texas in the early 19th century, often differing culturally and politically from Texicans. Why it matters: Their arrival introduced new dynamics and conflicts that affected Texican communities and Texas history.
- primary sources: Original historical documents and artifacts such as letters, legal records, and newspapers that provide firsthand evidence about the Texicans and Texas history. Why it matters: Primary sources are essential for accurate research and understanding of the Texicans’ lived experiences and historical context.
- terminal learning objective: The main capability learners should demonstrate by the end of the class. Why it matters: It anchors the lesson around outcomes instead of loose topic coverage.
- enabling learning objective: A supporting skill or idea learners need before they can meet the terminal objective. Why it matters: It turns a big outcome into teachable steps.
- source verification: An independent check that claims and citations are supported by the approved corpus. Why it matters: It keeps fabricated or unsupported material out of the class.
