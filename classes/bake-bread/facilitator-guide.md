# Facilitator Guide: bake bread

Quality score at build: 85 / 100 (strong)
Class tier: Quick briefing

## How to Run This Class
- Start by explaining the knowledge-base boundary and why the final works-cited slide matters.
- Use polls, word clouds, quizzes, and Bernard questions as participation signals.
- Treat deep dives as optional learner paths during delivery, but required preparation for the presenter.
- If a learner asks for a claim outside the source base, mark it as a research gap instead of improvising.

## Blueprint
- Orientation and learner baseline: 2 slides. Set the purpose, audience floor, assumptions, and mastery target.
- Knowledge base and source boundary: 3 slides. Show what the approved sources support, what is missing, and what must not be invented.
- Core concepts and vocabulary: 5 slides. Teach the essential terms, mental models, and decision points.
- Guided practice and examples: 6 slides. Work through realistic cases, common mistakes, checks, and facilitator prompts.
- Deep dives, edge cases, and quality risks: 6 slides. Add expert detail, safety cautions, disagreements, and advanced transfer examples.
- Assessment, transfer, and works cited: 7 slides. Prove mastery, capture participation, and close with source transparency.

## QA Notes
- Improve the knowledge base before relying on this as a finished masterclass.
