- Detailed primary source perspectives from Mexican military leaders and Tejano civilians during the revolution are limited in the current knowledge base.
- Extractable content from some secondary sources such as study.com, alamopecan.com, britannica.com, and briscoecenter.org is unavailable, reducing immediate usability.
- Multimedia analysis, such as from the YouTube source, is not fully integrated or summarized, limiting engagement with audiovisual content.
- In-depth coverage of the social and cultural impacts on indigenous peoples and enslaved African Americans during the revolution is sparse.
- More comprehensive coverage of the political aftermath and international relations following Texas independence would strengthen the knowledge base.
